Resident Evil 4 2005

About Resident Evil 4

Resident Evil 4 follows Leon S. Kennedy, now a U.S. government agent, on a mission to rescue the President’s daughter, Ashley Graham, from a mysterious cult in rural Spain. The game introduces a new type of enemy, Los Ganados, who are parasitically controlled villagers, offering a more intense and strategic combat experience compared to the traditional zombies of earlier Resident Evil games. With its over-the-shoulder camera perspective and fast-paced action, Resident Evil 4 revolutionized the survival horror genre. It blends intense combat, resource management, and environmental puzzles, taking players through eerie villages and castles. The game is widely praised for its thrilling gameplay and has been influential in shaping modern action-horror games.

Fun Fact #1

In Resident Evil 4, the game shifts away from the traditional T-virus zombie storyline and instead introduces a new parasite called Las Plagas. Unlike the virus-based infections seen in previous Resident Evil games, Las Plagas are parasites that attach themselves to a host's nervous system, allowing them to retain some intelligence and communication abilities, unlike the mindless zombies of earlier games. This made the enemies, known as Los Ganados, more frightening and unpredictable, as they could use weapons, set traps, and coordinate attacks against Leon. This shift in the story marked a major evolution in the Resident Evil series, bringing a fresh take on horror by combining intense action with unsettling, intelligent enemies.

Fun Fact #2

The Umbrella Corporation, with its iconic red and white umbrella logo, is one of the most recognizable symbols in Resident Evil. In Resident Evil 4, however, Umbrella Corporation plays a more background role in the story. By the time of Resident Evil 4’s events, Umbrella has already been dismantled due to its role in the Raccoon City incident and subsequent public exposure of its bioterror activities. Although Umbrella itself isn’t the main antagonist, its legacy lives on, as the Las Plagas parasite research and bioweapons in Resident Evil 4 can trace back to the influence of Umbrella's unethical experiments. The red umbrella logo remains a haunting reminder of the corporation's dark legacy, symbolizing the birth of bioterrorism that would continue to impact the series long after its fall.
